The Spanish Connection

Puerto Rican cuisine adopted a few Spanish dishes during the Spanish colonialization of Puerto Rico. From my own Puerto Rican upbringing, I learned that there was a a weekly menu of dishes we cooked and then there were the dishes only for Three Kings Day and other religious holidays. There are now more modern variations of dishes. For example, Pernil is specifically for Christmas or the Three Kings Day, but is now used for most holidays. Flan de Queso, which is Flan made of cream cheese is now Flancocho. Flancocho is a combination of Flan and Bizcocho (cake).
